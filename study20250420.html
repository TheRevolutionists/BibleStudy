Bible Study Session: April 20, 2025

Date: Saturday, 6:00 PM EST

Peak Attendance: 26

Scripture: James 1

Study Notes: The word err. To go astray, to wander.

Scripture Study

James 1:1 KJV

"James, a servant of God and of the Lord Jesus Christ, to the twelve tribes which are scattered abroad, greeting."

SOAP Analysis

Scripture: James 1:1

Observation: James identifies himself as a servant of God and Jesus Christ, addressing the dispersed Jewish Christians.

Application: We should embrace our role as servants of God, recognizing our position in His kingdom.

Prayer: Lord, help us to serve You faithfully as James did, with humility and dedication.

James 1:2-4 KJV

"My brethren, count it all joy when ye fall into divers temptations; Knowing this, that the trying of your faith worketh patience. But let patience have her perfect work, that ye may be perfect and entire, wanting nothing."

SOAP Analysis

Scripture: James 1:2-4

Observation: Trials should be viewed as opportunities for spiritual growth, developing patience and maturity.

Application: We should change our perspective on difficulties, seeing them as chances to grow stronger in faith.

Prayer: Father, help us to find joy in trials, knowing they develop our character and faith.

James 1:5-8 KJV

"If any of you lack wisdom, let him ask of God, that giveth to all men liberally, and upbraideth not; and it shall be given him. But let him ask in faith, nothing wavering. For he that wavereth is like a wave of the sea driven with the wind and tossed. For let not that man think that he shall receive any thing of the Lord. A double minded man is unstable in all his ways."

SOAP Analysis

Scripture: James 1:5-8

Observation: God freely gives wisdom to those who ask in faith, but doubt hinders our receiving from God.

Application: We should pray with confidence, avoiding doubt and maintaining steady faith in God's provision.

Prayer: Lord, increase our faith and help us to trust fully in Your wisdom and guidance.

James 1:9-11 KJV

"Let the brother of low degree rejoice in that he is exalted: But the rich, in that he is made low: because as the flower of the grass he shall pass away. For the sun is no sooner risen with a burning heat, but it withereth the grass, and the flower thereof falleth, and the grace of the fashion of it perisheth: so also shall the rich man fade away in his ways."

SOAP Analysis

Scripture: James 1:9-11

Observation: Both poverty and wealth are temporary conditions; true value lies in our spiritual position.

Application: We should find our identity in Christ, not in our economic or social status.

Prayer: God, help us to see our true worth in You, regardless of our earthly circumstances.

James 1:12 KJV

"Blessed is the man that endureth temptation: for when he is tried, he shall receive the crown of life, which the Lord hath promised to them that love him."

SOAP Analysis

Scripture: James 1:12

Observation: Enduring trials leads to eternal rewards; God promises blessings to those who persevere.

Application: We should maintain our faith during trials, focusing on the eternal reward.

Prayer: Lord, give us strength to endure trials, keeping our eyes on Your promises.

James 1:13-15 KJV

"Let no man say when he is tempted, I am tempted of God: for God cannot be tempted with evil, neither tempteth he any man: But every man is tempted, when he is drawn away of his own lust, and enticed. Then when lust hath conceived, it bringeth forth sin: and sin, when it is finished, bringeth forth death."

SOAP Analysis

Scripture: James 1:13-15

Observation: Temptation comes from our own desires, not from God. There's a progression from temptation to sin to death.

Application: We must recognize and resist our own sinful desires before they lead to destructive actions.

Prayer: Father, help us identify and overcome temptation through Your strength.

James 1:16-18 KJV

"Do not err, my beloved brethren. Every good gift and every perfect gift is from above, and cometh down from the Father of lights, with whom is no variableness, neither shadow of turning. Of his own will begat he us with the word of truth, that we should be a kind of firstfruits of his creatures."

SOAP Analysis

Scripture: James 1:16-18

Observation: All good things come from God, who is unchanging and the source of truth.

Application: We should recognize God's goodness in our lives and trust His unchanging nature.

Prayer: Thank You, Lord, for Your good gifts and unchanging character.

James 1:19-20 KJV

"Wherefore, my beloved brethren, let every man be swift to hear, slow to speak, slow to wrath: For the wrath of man worketh not the righteousness of God."

SOAP Analysis

Scripture: James 1:19-20

Observation: We should listen more than we speak and control our anger, as human anger doesn't produce God's righteousness.

Application: Practice being a better listener and controlling emotional reactions.

Prayer: Holy Spirit, help us to listen well and respond with patience rather than anger.

James 1:21-25 KJV

"Wherefore lay apart all filthiness and superfluity of naughtiness, and receive with meekness the engrafted word, which is able to save your souls. But be ye doers of the word, and not hearers only, deceiving your own selves. For if any be a hearer of the word, and not a doer, he is like unto a man beholding his natural face in a glass: For he beholdeth himself, and goeth his way, and straightway forgetteth what manner of man he was. But whoso looketh into the perfect law of liberty, and continueth therein, he being not a forgetful hearer, but a doer of the work, this man shall be blessed in his deed."

SOAP Analysis

Scripture: James 1:21-25

Observation: We must not only hear God's Word but also act on it. Simply hearing without doing is self-deception.

Application: Put God's Word into practice daily, not just listen to it.

Prayer: Lord, help us to be doers of Your Word, not just hearers.

James 1:26-27 KJV

"If any man among you seem to be religious, and bridleth not his tongue, but deceiveth his own heart, this man's religion is vain. Pure religion and undefiled before God and the Father is this, To visit the fatherless and widows in their affliction, and to keep himself unspotted from the world."

SOAP Analysis

Scripture: James 1:26-27

Observation: True religion involves controlling our speech, helping the needy, and maintaining personal purity.

Application: Practice faith through actions: control speech, help others, and avoid worldly corruption.

Prayer: Father, help us live out pure and undefiled religion through our actions and words.

Key Insights

  • The Lord allows us to be tempted to test our faith
  • It's easy to do good when everything is going well
  • Our faith is truly tested during hard times and negativity
  • We can use temptation as an opportunity to grow spiritually
  • We all make mistakes, but God loves us and we can turn to the Bible
