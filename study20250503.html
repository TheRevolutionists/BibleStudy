Scripture Study

James 2:14-26

Start: James 2:14 - 2:26

Key Notes

  • Be a doer of the word.
  • Someone can say "I have faith" but even demons believe; true faith is shown by works (v.18).
  • You can tell by their works who they are, not just their words.
  • You know about God, but are you actually following what He says?
  • Verse 24: Easy to misunderstand. The act of being declared righteous is used to refer to the wise being justified by their actions. If your faith does not produce fruit, it may be dead.
  • Verse 15: Luke 3:11 - Sometimes the Lord blesses us not solely for ourselves, but so we can bless others.
  • You will know a good tree and a bad tree by its fruit and its roots.

James 3:1-12

This passage is all about the power of the tongue and the importance of controlling what we say. James emphasizes that our words have a significant impact, both on ourselves and on others.

Notes & Summary

  • Verses 1-2: The Responsibility of Teachers
    James starts by cautioning those who desire to be teachers. He points out that teachers will be judged more strictly because they have a greater influence. He also notes that everyone stumbles in many ways, and if anyone could control their speech perfectly, they would be perfect in every way.
  • Verses 3-6: The Tongue as a Powerful Force
    James uses vivid metaphors: the tongue is like a bit in a horse's mouth or a rudder on a ship—small but powerful. The tongue can direct the course of our lives and is likened to a fire that can cause great destruction.
  • Verses 7-8: The Untamable Tongue
    Humans can tame all sorts of animals, but no one can tame the tongue. It is restless and evil, full of deadly poison.
  • Verses 9-12: The Inconsistency of Blessing and Cursing
    James points out the hypocrisy of using the tongue to praise God and then curse people made in God's image. This is unnatural, like a spring producing both fresh and salt water, or a fig tree bearing olives.
Summary: James 3:1-12 teaches us about the immense power and responsibility that comes with our words. It warns against the dangers of uncontrolled speech and calls us to strive for consistency between our faith and our communication.
