Bible Study Session: April 20, 2024

Date: Saturday, 6:00 PM EST

Peak Attendance: 26

Scripture: James 1:13-20

Study Notes: The word err. To go astray, to wander.

Scripture Study

James 1:13 KJV

"Let no man say when he is tempted, I am tempted of God: for God cannot be tempted with evil, neither tempteth he any man:"

SOAP Analysis

Scripture: James 1:13

Observation: This verse clearly states that God is not the source of temptation and cannot be tempted by evil Himself.

Application: When facing temptation, we should not blame God but recognize that temptations come from other sources.

Prayer: Lord, help us to recognize the true source of temptation and not attribute it to You. Give us wisdom to understand Your perfect nature.

James 1:14 KJV

"But every man is tempted, when he is drawn away of his own lust, and enticed."

SOAP Analysis

Scripture: James 1:14

Observation: Temptation comes from our own desires and lusts that draw us away from God's path.

Application: We must be aware of our own desires and recognize when they're leading us astray from God's will.

Prayer: Father, help us to recognize and control our own desires that lead us into temptation.

James 1:15 KJV

"Then when lust hath conceived, it bringeth forth sin: and sin, when it is finished, bringeth forth death."

SOAP Analysis

Scripture: James 1:15

Observation: There is a progression from lust to sin to death, showing the serious consequences of giving in to temptation.

Application: We must stop the cycle of sin at its beginning - when temptation first appears.

Prayer: Lord, give us strength to resist temptation at its earliest stages before it leads to sin and death.

James 1:19 KJV

"Wherefore, my beloved brethren, let every man be swift to hear, slow to speak, slow to wrath:"

SOAP Analysis

Scripture: James 1:19

Observation: We are instructed to listen more, speak less, and control our anger.

Application: Practice active listening and emotional control in our daily interactions.

Prayer: Holy Spirit, help us to be better listeners and to control our words and emotions.

Key Insights

  • The Lord allows us to be tempted to test our faith
  • It's easy to do good when everything is going well
  • Our faith is truly tested during hard times and negativity
  • We can use temptation as an opportunity to grow spiritually
  • We all make mistakes, but God loves us and we can turn to the Bible
